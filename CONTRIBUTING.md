# Contributing

Thank you for your interest. This repository holds a single-author, master's-level lecture series on deep learning for cybersecurity. The topics, teaching order, security framing, and content are authored and maintained by [Francisco Laplace](https://laplacef.me), so the most useful ways to take part are feedback, questions, and corrections rather than direct edits to the material.

By taking part you agree to abide by the [Code of Conduct](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

## Ways to take part

- **Discussions.** [GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/laplacef/deep-learning-for-cyber/discussions) is the main space. Use **Ideas** to suggest a topic or broader coverage, **Q&A** to ask about a concept, **Show and tell** to share how you have used or adapted the material, and **General** for anything else.
- **Issues.** [Open an issue](https://github.com/laplacef/deep-learning-for-cyber/issues) for concrete corrections: a typo, a broken link, a factual or citation error, or a rendering bug in a deck. Please search existing issues first, and name the deck and slide where you can.

## Pull requests

This is an authored teaching resource, not a collaboratively developed codebase, so it does not take pull requests that change deck content. A single author keeps the voice consistent across the series and keeps the licensing clean (see below).

Small mechanical fixes such as a typo or a dead link are welcome, but please open an issue or a discussion first so the fix can be confirmed before you send anything. Unsolicited content pull requests will be redirected to Discussions.

## Using and adapting the material

If you build on the decks or borrow the presentation style, a note in **Show and tell** is welcome so others can find your work. For commercial licensing, see [LICENSE.md](./LICENSE.md).

## Reporting a concern

For conduct concerns, contact the maintainer through the form at <https://laplacef.me/contact>, as described in the [Code of Conduct](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
